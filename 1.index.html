üìÖ DAY -3: PREPARE OLIVINE (Allow 2-3 hours + overnight drying)
2 ü™® PREPARE OLIVINE MINERAL SUBSTRATE
‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: Olivine must be clean, right size, and sterilized properly. This is the ONLY source of iron for bacteria. Don't rush this step!

STEP 2A: Crush Olivine to Right Size

2A.1
Get your olivine sample
  • Use forsterite-rich olivine (Fo88 or similar)
  • If you have large chunks, you need to crush them
  • You'll need approximately 10-15 grams total for all experiments
2A.2
Clean mortar and pestle thoroughly
  • Wash with DI water
  • Rinse 3-5 times
  • Let air dry
2A.3
Place olivine in mortar
  • Add small amount at a time (2-3 grams)
  • Don't overfill - need room to crush
2A.4
Crush gently by hand
  • Use grinding motion with pestle
  • Don't pound hard - gentle grinding works better
  • Crush until most pieces are small (like coarse sand)
  • This takes 5-10 minutes per batch

STEP 2B: Sieve to 150-300 Œºm Size

2B.1
Get sieves ready:
  • You need: 150 Œºm sieve (bottom) + 300 Œºm sieve (top)
  • Stack them: 300 Œºm on top, 150 Œºm below, collection pan on bottom
üí° What's happening: Particles >300 Œºm stay on top sieve (too big - crush more). Particles 150-300 Œºm collect on middle sieve (PERFECT - keep these!). Particles <150 Œºm fall through to pan (too small - discard).
2B.2
Pour crushed olivine onto top (300 Œºm) sieve
2B.3
Gently shake and tap sieves for 2-3 minutes
  • Use side-to-side motion
  • Tap edges occasionally
  • Continue until no more particles fall through
2B.4
Collect particles from 150 Œºm sieve (middle one)
  • These are 150-300 Œºm = PERFECT SIZE!
  • Transfer to clean container
  • Set aside
2B.5
Handle other fractions:
  • Particles on 300 Œºm sieve (top) = TOO BIG ‚Üí crush more and re-sieve
  • Particles in bottom pan = TOO SMALL ‚Üí discard
2B.6
Repeat until you have ~10 grams of 150-300 Œºm olivine

STEP 2C: Wash Olivine 7 Times (CRITICAL!)

üö´ CRITICAL: You MUST wash 7 times! This removes clay particles and dust that will interfere with your results. Yes, 7 times. Don't skip any washes!
2C.1
Get ready:
  • Clean 250 mL beaker
  • Ultrasonicator (sonication bath)
  • 200-proof ethanol (you'll need ~500 mL total)
  • Timer
2C.2
WASH #1:
  • Put olivine (150-300 Œºm) in beaker
  • Add 200-proof ethanol until olivine is covered (about 100 mL)
  • Place beaker in ultrasonicator
  • Turn on ultrasonicator
  • Sonicate for EXACTLY 5 minutes
  • After 5 min, carefully decant (pour off) ethanol into waste container
  • Keep olivine in beaker!
2C.3
WASH #2:
  • Add fresh ethanol to same beaker
  • Sonicate 5 minutes
  • Decant ethanol
2C.4
WASH #3: Repeat same process
2C.5
WASH #4: Repeat same process
2C.6
WASH #5: Repeat same process
2C.7
WASH #6: Repeat same process
2C.8
WASH #7 (FINAL): Repeat same process
üí° What you'll notice: After 7 washes, the ethanol should be much clearer. Early washes = cloudy ethanol (lots of fine particles). Final washes = clear ethanol (clean olivine!).

STEP 2D: Dry Olivine

2D.1
After 7th wash, decant ethanol as much as possible
2D.2
Spread olivine in thin layer
  • Can keep in beaker OR
  • Pour onto clean aluminum foil or glass petri dish
  • Spread evenly
2D.3
Place in drying oven at 130¬∞C
2D.4
Dry OVERNIGHT (12-16 hours)
  • Ethanol must evaporate completely
  • Leave oven on overnight
  • Check next morning
2D.5
Next morning (Day -2):
  • Turn off oven
  • Let cool to room temperature (~1 hour)
  • Transfer dry olivine to clean, sealed container
  • Label: "Washed Olivine 150-300 Œºm - Date"

üìÖ DAY -3: ACID-WASH CONTAINERS (Overnight process)
3 üßº ACID-WASH ALL CONTAINERS
üö´ SAFETY FIRST! You'll work with 10% hydrochloric acid (HCl). Wear: safety goggles, lab coat, and gloves. Work in fume hood if possible.

What you need to acid-wash:

  • 3 Erlenmeyer flasks (150 mL) with porous caps ‚Üí for Exp 1
  • 15 serum bottles (120-160 mL) ‚Üí for Exp 2A, 2B, 3A, 3B + controls
  • Sample tubes and pipettes
  • ALL polycarbonate or polypropylene (NO GLASS!)

STEP 3A: Prepare 10% HCl Solution

3A.1
Calculate how much you need:
  • You need enough to submerge all containers
  • Probably ~2-3 liters total
3A.2
Get large plastic container or bucket
  • Must be acid-resistant (polypropylene is good)
  • Big enough to fit all your containers
3A.3
Make 10% HCl solution:
Formula: Always add acid to water (NOT water to acid!)
For 2 liters of 10% HCl:
- Add 1800 mL DI water to bucket FIRST
- Then SLOWLY add 200 mL concentrated HCl (usually 37%)
- Swirl gently
- Let cool (mixing generates heat!)
‚ö†Ô∏è NEVER add water to acid! This can cause violent splashing and burns. Always add acid to water slowly!

STEP 3B: Wash Containers

3B.1
Pre-rinse all containers with DI water
  • Rinse inside and outside
  • Shake out excess water
3B.2
Submerge all containers in 10% HCl solution
  • Make sure they're fully submerged
  • If they float, weight them down
  • Include caps/stoppers!
3B.3
Cover bucket with lid or foil
3B.4
Let soak OVERNIGHT (12-24 hours)
  • Put in fume hood if possible
  • Put sign: "Acid Bath - Do Not Disturb"
  • Leave overnight
3B.5
Next day: Remove containers from acid
  • Wear gloves!
  • Take out one by one
  • Let acid drip back into bucket
3B.6
Rinse THOROUGHLY with DI water
  • Rinse each container 5-7 times
  • Fill with water, shake, pour out
  • Repeat until pH is neutral (test with pH paper)
  • No acid smell should remain
3B.7
Air dry in clean area
  • Place upside down on clean surface
  • Let dry completely (few hours)
3B.8
Autoclave all dried containers
  • Place caps loosely on containers
  • Autoclave at 121¬∞C for 20 minutes
  • Dry cycle if available
3B.9
After autoclaving:
  • Let cool completely
  • Tighten caps
  • Store sealed until use
  • Keep in clean area
üí° Why acid-wash? Removes all trace metal contamination. Even tiny amounts of metal on container walls can interfere with your iron-limited experiments!
